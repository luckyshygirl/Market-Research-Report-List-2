<p><p>Trichlorosilane (TCS) is a chemical compound used in the production of polysilicon, a key material for the manufacturing of solar cells and semiconductors. TCS is primarily produced through two different market types: the Direct Chlorination (DC) Process and the Hydrochlorination (HC) Process. In the DC process, high-purity silicon is reacted with chlorine gas to produce TCS. Conversely, the HC process involves reacting metallurgical grade silicon with hydrogen chloride to obtain TCS. Both market types play a crucial role in supplying TCS for the polysilicon industry.</p></p>
