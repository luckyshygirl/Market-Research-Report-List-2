<p><p>Non-Agriculture Smart Irrigation systems are categorized into two main types, namely Weather-based Controllers and Sensor-based Controllers. Weather-based Controllers use real-time weather data to optimize irrigation schedules, taking into account factors such as rainfall, humidity, and temperature. This helps to ensure that irrigation is only done when necessary, conserving water and reducing waste. On the other hand, Sensor-based Controllers use various sensors to monitor soil moisture levels, temperature, and other environmental conditions, allowing for precise and efficient irrigation based on actual plant needs. Both types of controllers provide smart and automated solutions for water management in non-agricultural settings.</p></p>
