<p><p>Polyethylene plastic compounding refers to the process of combining polyethylene resin with various additives to enhance its properties. The market is categorized into three types: high-density polyethylene (HDPE), linear low-density polyethylene (LLDPE), and low-density polyethylene (LDPE). HDPE is known for its strong and rigid nature, making it suitable for applications like pipes and bottles. LLDPE is more flexible and tear-resistant, making it ideal for films and bags. LDPE, on the other hand, is the most flexible and has excellent clarity, making it suitable for packaging and wraps.</p></p>
