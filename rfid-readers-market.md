<p><p>RFID (Radio Frequency Identification) Readers are categorized into different types based on the frequency at which they operate. LF (Low Frequency) RFID readers typically operate at 125-135 kHz, suitable for short-range applications such as access control systems. HF (High Frequency) RFID readers operate at 13.56 MHz, offering moderate read range and commonly used for payment systems and document tracking. UHF (Ultra-High Frequency) RFID readers operate at 860-960 MHz, providing long-range capabilities used in supply chain management and inventory tracking. MW (Microwave) RFID readers operate at high frequencies above 1 GHz, enabling very high-speed data transfer for specialized applications.</p></p>
