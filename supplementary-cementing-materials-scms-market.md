<p><p>Supplementary Cementing Materials (SCMs) are materials that are used in combination with cement in concrete to improve its performance and durability. The use of SCMs helps in reducing the consumption of cement, which is one of the major contributors to carbon emissions in the construction sector. Firstly, there is a shift towards the use of more environmentally friendly SCMs, such as fly ash, silica fume, and ground granulated blast furnace slag, which have a lower carbon footprint compared to traditional cement. <p><p>Supplementary Cementing Materials (SCMs) are materials added to cement to enhance its properties. Fly ash, slag cement, silica fume, and quartz flour are types of SCMs. Fly ash is a byproduct of coal combustion, slag cement is produced from iron ore waste, silica fume is a byproduct of silicon metal production, and quartz flour is a finely ground form of quartz. These materials are used in construction to improve the strength, durability, and workability of concrete. They also contribute to sustainable construction practices by reducing the amount of cement required and reducing waste generation.</p></p>
