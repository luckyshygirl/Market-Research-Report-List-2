<p><p>The blood sensor market consists of two types, namely minimally invasive and non-invasive. Minimally invasive blood sensors involve a slight penetration of the skin to obtain blood samples for measurement. This method is commonly used in glucose monitoring devices. On the other hand, non-invasive blood sensors do not require any physical penetration of the skin and measure blood parameters through external devices. These sensors are used in wearable devices such as smartwatches and fitness trackers. Both types offer different approaches for obtaining blood-related data for various medical and personal health monitoring purposes.</p></p>
