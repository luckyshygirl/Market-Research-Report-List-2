<p><p>Cartilage regeneration refers to the process of repairing damaged cartilage in joints. There are various methods used in this field. Microfracture involves creating small holes in the damaged area to stimulate new tissue growth. Debridement involves removing damaged cartilage to promote healing. Abrasion arthroplasty smoothens the damaged surface. Autologous chondrocyte implantation involves harvesting and implanting healthy cartilage cells from the patient. Osteochondral autograft transplantation uses healthy cartilage from the patient's own body for transplantation. Osteochondral allograft transplantation involves using healthy cartilage from a donor. Cell-based cartilage resurfacing uses cells grown in a lab to replace damaged cartilage. Other methods may also be used in the cartilage regeneration market.</p></p>
