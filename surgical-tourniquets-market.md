<p><p>Surgical tourniquets are medical devices used to temporarily restrict blood flow to a specific area of the body during surgical procedures. They are often used in orthopedic surgeries, where they can help create a bloodless surgical field, reducing the risk of bleeding and enabling surgeons to perform operations more effectively. Surgical tourniquets are typically inflatable cuffs that are placed around a patient's limb, and they are connected to a pressure device that allows the surgeon to control the amount of pressure applied.</p><p>The surgical tourniquets market is witnessing significant growth due to factors such as increasing surgical procedures, rising prevalence of orthopedic injuries and diseases, and technological advancements in tourniquet systems. Additionally, the growing adoption of minimally invasive surgeries is also contributing to market growth, as surgical tourniquets play a crucial role in such procedures.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is expected to witness a CAGR of 11.3% during the forecast period, indicating a positive growth trend. This growth can be attributed to the increasing demand for advanced surgical tourniquet systems, which offer improved safety features and enhanced patient comfort.</p><p>The latest trends in the surgical tourniquets market include the development of digitally integrated tourniquet systems that enable real-time monitoring and precise control of pressure levels. These systems provide surgeons with valuable data and offer improved patient outcomes. Additionally, the introduction of disposable tourniquet cuffs and non-pneumatic tourniquet systems is also gaining traction in the market, as they reduce the risk of cross-contamination and offer enhanced patient comfort.</p><p>In conclusion, the surgical tourniquets market is experiencing significant growth due to increasing surgical procedures, technological advancements, and the rising demand for minimally invasive surgeries.
