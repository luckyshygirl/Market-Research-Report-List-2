<p><p>Passive temperature-controlled packaging refers to the use of insulated shippers, insulated containers, refrigerants, and other methods to maintain desired temperature ranges during the transportation of goods. Insulated shippers are packaging solutions designed to provide thermal protection and prevent heat exchange between the product and the external environment. Insulated containers are larger storage units equipped with insulation materials to maintain a controlled temperature for a longer duration. Refrigerants are cooling agents used to maintain the desired temperature inside the packaging, such as gel packs or dry ice. Other methods may include phase change materials or vacuum insulation panels to achieve temperature control.</p></p>
