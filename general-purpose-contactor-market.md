<p><p>The general purpose contactor market consists of two main types: DC contactors and AC contactors. DC contactors are designed to control direct current (DC) circuits and are commonly used in battery-powered applications. They are capable of handling high voltage and current levels. On the other hand, AC contactors are specifically designed for alternating current (AC) circuits. They are commonly used in industrial and commercial applications to control motors and other electrical loads. AC contactors can handle high voltages and currents, but they require additional components like suppressors and arc chutes for proper operation.</p></p>
