<p><p>Ultrasound imaging refers to the use of sound waves to create images inside the body. Different types of ultrasound imaging techniques are utilized depending on the purpose and desired level of detail. These include 2D imaging which produces two-dimensional flat images, 3D imaging that creates three-dimensional images, and 4D imaging which adds motion to the three-dimensional images. Doppler imaging assesses blood flow within the body. The "Others" market category represents additional specialized ultrasound imaging techniques like elastography or contrast-enhanced imaging that provide specific diagnostic information.</p></p>
