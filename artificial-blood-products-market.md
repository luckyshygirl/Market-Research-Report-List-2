<p><p>Artificial blood products, specifically Perfluorocarbon-based Oxygen Carrier (PFBOC) and Hemoglobin-based Oxygen Carrier (HBOC), are types of blood substitutes used to carry oxygen to the body's tissues when natural blood is unavailable or insufficient. PFBOCs are synthetic compounds that dissolve and transport oxygen, while HBOCs are solutions made from modified human or animal hemoglobin. Both products are designed to mimic the function of natural blood by improving oxygen delivery, making them potentially valuable for emergency transfusions and medical procedures.</p></p>
