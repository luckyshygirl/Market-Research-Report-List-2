<p><p>The Continuous Dynamic Disturbance Recorder is a type of market where the recorder continuously monitors and records any disturbances or irregularities in a system. It provides real-time data and allows for continuous analysis and monitoring. On the other hand, the Triggered Dynamic Disturbance Recorder is a market where the recorder is triggered to record data only when a predefined disturbance or event occurs. It helps capture specific events of interest and is useful for targeted analysis and problem-solving. Both types offer valuable insights into system performance and help in identifying and addressing issues.</p></p>
