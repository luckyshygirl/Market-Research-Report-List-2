<p><p>Non-clog pumps are used to move fluids without getting clogged by solids. Two common types in the market are vortex and impeller pumps. Vortex pumps work by creating a vortex or whirlpool effect, which allows the solids to pass through without causing clogging. On the other hand, impeller pumps use rotating blades or vanes to move the fluid, effectively preventing the solids from obstructing the flow. Both types offer efficient and reliable solutions for handling fluids with solid particles, ensuring smooth operation and minimal maintenance.</p></p>
