<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Pulsed Xenon Lamps are commonly classified into two types, Short Arc Light and Long Arc Light. Short Arc Light lamps are characterized by a small, focused electrode gap and a short distance between the anode and cathode. They produce intense, concentrated light, making them suitable for applications requiring high brightness and precision, like projection systems and lithography tools. On the other hand, Long Arc Light lamps have a larger electrode gap and a longer distance between the anode and cathode. They generate a broader beam of light and are often used in applications like microscopy and fluorescence imaging.</p></p>
