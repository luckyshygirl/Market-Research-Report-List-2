<p><p>An Implanted Venous Access Device (IVAD), also known as implantable ports or port-a-caths, is a medical device that provides long-term access to blood vessels for administering medications, chemotherapy, or drawing blood samples. It is surgically placed under the skin, usually in the upper chest or arm, and is connected to a catheter that is inserted into a central vein.</p><p>The Implanted Venous Access Device Market is expected to witness steady growth during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, such as cancer and cardiovascular diseases, is one of the primary factors driving market growth. IVADs offer convenience and reduced discomfort to patients undergoing frequent or long-term treatments, contributing to their growing adoption.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements in IVADs, such as the development of needle-free systems and antimicrobial devices, are further fueling market growth. These advancements aim to improve patient comfort, reduce infection risks, and enhance the overall usability of the devices.</p><p>In addition, rising healthcare expenditure and infrastructure development in emerging economies are creating favorable market conditions. Increasing awareness about the benefits of IVADs among healthcare professionals and patients is also boosting market growth.</p><p>However, the high cost of implantation and potential complications associated with the use of IVADs, such as infection, thrombosis, and device malfunction, can hinder market growth. <p><p>The Implanted Venous Access Device market consists of different types of medical devices used for long-term intravenous treatment. Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICC) are inserted in the arm and threaded through to the superior vena cava. Centrally Inserted Catheters can be non-tunnelled or tunnelled, depending on their insertion method. Implanted Ports are placed under the skin and connected to a catheter. Other devices, such as subcutaneous ports or tunneled external catheters, are also used. These devices provide a reliable means of administering medication and fluids directly into the bloodstream.</p></p>
