<p><ul><li>Electrophilic Fluorinating Reagents</li><li>Nucleophilic Fluorinating Reagents</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Fluorinating Reagents market can be classified into two types: Electrophilic Fluorinating Reagents and Nucleophilic Fluorinating Reagents. Electrophilic Fluorinating Reagents are reagents that contain electrophilic fluorine, meaning they can donate a fluorine atom to react with other substances. These reagents are commonly used for the introduction of fluorine into organic molecules. On the other hand, Nucleophilic Fluorinating Reagents are reagents that contain nucleophilic fluorine, meaning they can accept a fluorine atom during a chemical reaction. These reagents are typically used for nucleophilic fluorination reactions.</p></p>
