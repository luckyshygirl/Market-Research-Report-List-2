<p><p>Influenza antiviral drugs are medications used to treat influenza infections caused by different strains of the influenza virus. These drugs can be classified into two types: neuraminidase inhibitors (NAIs) and adamantanes. Neuraminidase inhibitors prevent the release and spread of newly formed virus particles from infected cells, while adamantanes block the ion channel activity of the virus, inhibiting its replication process.</p><p>The market for influenza antiviral drugs is expected to witness significant growth in the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of influenza infections, along with the rising awareness among people about the importance of early diagnosis and treatment, is driving the demand for these drugs.
