<p><p>Biocompatible materials are substances that are compatible with living tissues and can be used safely in medical applications. Synthetic polymers are man-made materials that are designed to have specific properties and functionalities. Synthetic biocompatible commodity polymers are mass-produced synthetic polymers used in medical devices. Natural biocompatible polymers are derived from natural sources such as proteins or polysaccharides. Biocompatible ceramics are inorganic materials often used in dental implants or bone replacements. Biocompatible metals, like titanium or stainless steel, are metals that have been modified to have desirable biocompatibility properties for medical implants or devices.</p></p>
