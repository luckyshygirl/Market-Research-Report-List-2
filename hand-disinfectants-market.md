<p><p>Hand disinfectants are available in two main types: alcohol-based and non-alcohol-based. Alcohol-based hand disinfectants contain ethanol, isopropanol, or n-propanol as active ingredients. These types are effective in killing various microorganisms and are commonly used in healthcare settings. On the other hand, non-alcohol-based hand disinfectants typically contain chemicals like benzalkonium chloride or triclosan. They work by disrupting the cell membranes of bacteria and other germs. While alcohol-based types are generally preferred due to their higher efficacy, non-alcohol-based types are often utilized in settings where alcohol is not suitable, such as schools or for sensitive skin types.</p></p>
