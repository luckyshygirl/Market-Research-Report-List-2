<p><p>Agri-PV system, also known as agrivoltaic system or solar farming, is a concept that combines agricultural production with solar panel installations on the same piece of land. The idea behind this system is to maximize land use efficiency by generating solar energy while maintaining agricultural activities below.</p><p>The market for Agri-PV systems is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing global demand for renewable energy sources, coupled with the need to optimize land use, is driving the adoption of Agri-PV systems. These systems offer numerous benefits such as increased crop yield, reduced water requirements, and improved energy generation.</p><p>The integration of solar panels in Agri-PV systems allows for better utilization of available resources. The panels provide shade for the crops, reducing water evaporation and the risk of heat stress. In turn, the shade from the plants helps in maintaining optimum temperature for the solar panels, leading to improved energy generation. This mutually beneficial setup has attracted the attention of farmers, governments, and energy companies alike, further boosting the market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the trend towards sustainable agriculture practices and the implementation of supportive government policies are further propelling the adoption of Agri-PV systems. With the growing focus on reducing carbon footprints and promoting clean energy sources, Agri-PV systems offer a viable solution for sustainable energy production without compromising food production.</p><p>In conclusion, the Agri-PV system market is witnessing steady growth and is expected to continue its momentum in the forecast period. The integration of solar panels with agricultural activities not only optimizes land use but also offers a sustainable approach to energy generation. The market is projected to grow at a Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 5.7% during the forecast period.</p></p>

<p><p>The Agri-PV system market comprises of two main types: Ground System and Aquatic System. In the Ground System market, solar panels are installed on the ground in agricultural fields, allowing both crops and solar energy to be harvested simultaneously. On the other hand, Aquatic System involves the installation of solar panels on bodies of water such as ponds or reservoirs. This type of system not only generates electricity but also helps reduce water evaporation. Both systems provide renewable energy solutions while utilizing land and water resources efficiently in agricultural settings.</p></p>
