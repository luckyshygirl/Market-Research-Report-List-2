<p><p>The High Integrity Pressure Protection System (HIPPS) is divided into two main types: electronic system and mechanical system. The electronic system utilizes sensors and programmable logic controllers to monitor pressure levels and trigger safety measures when a threshold is exceeded. It is efficient, accurate, and can be integrated into control systems. On the other hand, the mechanical system relies on mechanical components such as valves and springs to sense pressure changes and take necessary protective actions. These devices are robust and reliable but may require manual adjustment and maintenance.</p></p>
