<p><p>Presulfurizing catalysts can be categorized into two types based on their catalytic activity. Non-catalytic activity refers to catalysts that are unable to induce chemical reactions on their own and rely solely on external factors for the sulfurization process. On the other hand, weak catalytic activity describes catalysts that possess some level of ability to initiate chemical reactions but are not as effective as other catalysts in promoting the sulfurizing process. Both types play a role in the presulfurizing catalyst market, but their effectiveness varies.</p></p>
