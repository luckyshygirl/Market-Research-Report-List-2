<p><p>A Voltage Controlled Oscillator (VCO) is an electronic component that generates a continuous varying output frequency depending on the input voltage. The market for VCOs is categorized into Quartz Oscillator and Silicon Oscillator. Quartz Oscillators are based on the piezoelectric effect of quartz crystals and are widely used in high-precision applications like telecommunication systems. On the other hand, Silicon Oscillators use semiconductor devices, offering lower cost and smaller size, making them suitable for consumer electronics and low-precision applications. Both types serve different market needs based on their accuracy, stability, and cost requirements.</p></p>
