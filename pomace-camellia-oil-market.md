<p><p>Pomace Camellia Oil is categorized into two market types: expelling and lixiviation process. </p><p>In the expelling process, the oil is extracted from the pomace (the residue left after pressing seeds) through mechanical means, such as crushing or pressing. This method is straightforward and cost-effective.</p><p>On the other hand, the lixiviation process involves using solvents, such as hexane, to dissolve and separate the oil from the pomace. It is a more complex and expensive extraction method, commonly used for obtaining higher oil yields or when the expelling process is not feasible.</p><p>Both market types offer different extraction techniques for obtaining Pomace Camellia Oil.</p></p>
