<p><p>Zebrafish (Danio rerio) is a popular tropical freshwater fish species commonly used in scientific research, particularly in the field of genetics and developmental biology. The increasing use of zebrafish in drug discovery and development for testing potential therapeutics is driving the market growth. Zebrafish are used to test the efficacy and safety of drugs, as well as to study their effects on specific disease models. They are particularly useful in screening large numbers of compounds quickly and cost-effectively.</p><p>Another factor contributing to market growth is the rising demand for zebrafish for educational purposes. Zebrafish are commonly used in schools and universities for teaching biology and genetics due to their ease of care and their ability to demonstrate various biological concepts.</p><p>The market is also witnessing the emergence of genetically modified zebrafish strains, which are bred to mimic specific human diseases or conditions. These strains allow researchers to study the genetic basis of diseases and develop potential treatments. This development is expected to further fuel the growth of the zebrafish market.</p><p>In terms of trends, there is an increasing focus on the development of advanced zebrafish research facilities, including zebrafish laboratories and aquaculture systems. These facilities are designed to provide optimal conditions for zebrafish breeding and maintenance, ensuring high-quality research outcomes.</p><p>Furthermore, there is a growing trend towards the adoption of automated systems and robotics in zebrafish research. These technologies enable efficient handling and monitoring of zebrafish populations, saving time and improving research productivity.</p><p>Overall, the zebrafish market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 5.6% during the forecast period, driven by the increasing utilization of zebrafish in drug discovery, education, and the development of genetically modified strains.
