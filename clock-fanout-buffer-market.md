LVPECL (low-voltage positive emitter-coupled logic) offers high-speed performance and low output jitter. LVDS (low-voltage differential signaling) is known for its low power consumption and noise immunity. HCSL (high-speed current steering logic) is commonly used in memory applications. CML (current-mode logic) provides high-speed, low-power solutions for high-speed serial data transmission. HSTL (high-speed transceiver logic) is suitable for high-speed memory interfaces. Each type addresses specific requirements for clock distribution and signal transmission in different applications.</p></p>
